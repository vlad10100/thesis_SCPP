Solar Chimney Power Plant

The solar chimney power plant (SCPP) that generates power through solar energy with a material that stores heat naturally; and in the limited availability of sunlight or nighttime gradually realease heat to further produce energy. The main elements of SCPP are: turbine generator, solar chimney, solar collector and solar roof. The components of SCPP help convert energy in the form of convection to produce electricity via turbine. When natural air flows through the solar collector’s inlet, the heat/solar energy stored by the collector is slowly released thus heating up the air. Afterwards, the heated air expands and becomes less dense causing it to flow upward to the collector’s outlet into the chimney’s inlet where the turbine is located. The upward draft of air caused by heat convection serves as the driving force of the turbine. SCPP works naturally as the air is its working fluid, and the sun heats the air in the collector, which causes it to warm the air

This study focuses on the construction of a small-scale prototype and testing the generated power with sensible material to store solar energy. The aim of the study is to determine and sort the sensible heat storage materials that will produce the greatest amount of energy. The sensible heat storage material is anticipated to release its stored heat at night where there is high demand for electricity and when sunlight is unavailable to heat the surrounding. The data will be gathered by performing three trials in each sensible heat storage materials (pebble, sand, combination of pebble and sand), the data to gather are date, time, temperature, and wind speed. The data will be use to give visualization and conclusion regarding the optimum sensible heat storage material to be use by comparing the peak output loads and efficiencies.
